    03 · GUIDE

    How to use DAIS-10

    Complete walkthrough from upload to results. Follow these steps for the most accurate governance analysis.

    Step 1 — Prepare your data file
    Start with a clean CSV or JSON file. DAIS-10 works best when column names are descriptive — patient_id rather than col1.
    GOOD COLUMN NAMES
    patient_id
    date_of_birth
    diagnosis_code
    admission_date
    treating_physician
    AVOID
    col1, col2, col3
    field_a, field_b
    x, y, z
    data1, data2
    untitled
    Free tier: up to 999 rows, 19 columns, 1MB · Pro: up to 49,999 rows, 199 columns, 10MB
    Step 2 — Upload and select your domain
    Drop your file onto the upload zone. Select the domain that matches your dataset — this is the single most important configuration decision. Domain determines which fields DAIS-10 treats as Essential.
    Healthcare
    HIPAA standard
    Finance
    SOX standard
    E-commerce
    PCI-DSS standard
    ML / AI
    EU AI Act standard
    Step 3 — Define your column schema
    After upload, every detected column appears in a table with DAIS-10 inferred tier and importance — shown greyed until you confirm them. Review each row and adjust if the inference is wrong for your dataset.
    E
    Essential — importance ~95
    Missing value collapses row score. Use for primary identifiers and legally required fields.
    EC
    Semi-Essential — importance ~80
    Important but conditional. Missing value significantly reduces row score.
    C
    Contextual — importance ~58
    Adds context. Missing reduces score moderately.
    N
    Enrichment — importance ~18
    Metadata or audit fields. Missing has minimal impact on score.
    Use Confirm All to accept all inferred values at once, or confirm each row individually. At least one confirmed column is required to proceed.
    Step 4 — Select compliance standards and retention
    Tick the compliance standards you want to evaluate against. Your domain pre-selects the most likely standard. Selecting a standard with a fixed retention period auto-fills the years field.
    GDPR — leave retention years blank or enter your organisation's purpose-specific period.
    HIPAA — auto-fills 6 years (§164.530(j) minimum).
    SOX — auto-fills 7 years (Section 802).
    PCI-DSS — auto-fills 1 year online.
    EU AI Act — auto-fills 10 years (Art.12).
    IEC 61508 — enter your system's operational life plus 10 years.
    Set the timestamp column if your dataset has a creation date — this enables temporal decay scoring so older rows receive lower freshness scores.
    Step 5 — Run and read your results
    Click Proceed to Analysis then Run DAIS-10 Analysis. Results appear in 8 tabs:
    COLUMN SCHEMA
    Tier, importance, AMD pass rate, VCL validity score per column
    BEST / WORST ROWS
    Top and bottom rows by governance score with top contributor
    CAP EVENTS
    Rows where one field is bottlenecking the score — missing, invalid, or expired
    AMD DIAGNOSTICS
    22 automated tests per column — pass, warn, fail breakdown
    CONFORMANCE
    Gap analysis against selected standards — present vs missing mandatory fields
    CCM
    Cross-column contradictions — date logic, age/DOB, negative amounts
    Download results as Full JSON, Schema YAML, or Row Scores CSV (Pro tier). Request a certified PDF report via email.
